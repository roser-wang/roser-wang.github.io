水墨画 Ink Painting

Ink painting is a style of Chinese traditional painting, flourished from the Tang Dynasty. With the simple tools of brush, ink, and paper, China’s scholars and artists brought out “the beauty between resemblance and non-resemblance.” The style emphasizes the perceived "spirit" or "essence" of a subject over direct imitation.
Landscape painting（山水画） is one of the most famous topic in Chinese traditional painting, composed of two subjects mainly: the water （水）and the mountain (山).

汉字 Chinese Characters

Compared to English, the biggest difference between these two languages is that each Chinese character or word has an individual meaning itself. The characters are derived from shapes of the subjects, as an abstraction of the physical world.
A Chinese radical or indexing component is a graphical component of a Chinese character under which the character is traditionally listed in a Chinese dictionary. Most of the time, the radical indicates the underlined meaning or partial image of a charater. For example, "山" as a radical means "mountains" and "氵" as a radical means "water."
